Risk Zones in Sikkim

Be aware of landslide-prone areas and restricted zones before planning your trip.

Landslide-Prone Areas

North Sikkim, Gangtok outskirts, and hill roads after heavy rain. Avoid traveling during monsoon.

Restricted Areas

Some border areas in North and West Sikkim require special permits. Check with local authorities.

High Altitude

Places above 12,000 ft may cause altitude sickness. Acclimatize gradually and stay hydrated.

Wildlife & Road Safety

Drive carefully in forest areas; watch out for animals crossing roads and slippery terrain.

General Safety Tips:

  • Check weather forecasts before travel.
  • Carry emergency contacts and first aid kit.
  • Travel with certified guides for high-altitude treks.
  • Follow local rules and avoid restricted zones.

Local Transport in Sikkim

Since online transport often doesn’t work, here’s how to move around Sikkim safely and efficiently.

Shared Taxis

Most common mode of transport connecting towns and tourist spots. Economical and reliable.

Tip: Depart when full; confirm fare with driver before boarding.

State Buses

Run between major cities like Gangtok, Pelling, Namchi, and Mangan.

Tip: Check schedule at local bus stands; tickets sold offline.

Private Taxis

Hire a private taxi for day trips or remote areas. Best for families or groups.

Tip: Negotiate price before starting; always ask for driver ID.

Jeep Services

Used for hilly or remote routes; depart once full. Affordable and adventurous.

Tip: Keep small cash ready; travel light for comfort.

General Tips for Local Transport:

  • Carry cash; digital payments may not work in remote areas.
  • Start early for long journeys to avoid night travel on mountain roads.
  • Always confirm fares and routes before departure.
  • Keep emergency contacts and local guide info handy.
